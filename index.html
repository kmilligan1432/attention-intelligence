Perspective

I've seen this
shift before.

In 2008, while many agencies were struggling, we leaned early into content marketing—before it was widely adopted.

While competitors were losing ground, our business grew.

Not because we worked harder. Because we saw the shift early—and built around it.

I see the same pattern happening now with AI.

Most people are using it for speed. Very few are using it to think more clearly and operate differently.

That's where the real advantage is.
Attention Intelligence

Why Attention
Comes First.

As AI reshapes how we work and live, many are calling this the era of relational intelligence.

I agree that relationships matter more than ever.

But in my experience, the real bottleneck is attention.

Because when your attention is fragmented, everything else suffers. Your decisions. Your relationships. Your ability to lead.

Before we can communicate better, lead better, or show up better for the people we care about, we have to be able to see clearly what is competing for our attention in the first place.

That is the work. That is where AI can help.

Not by replacing judgment, but by helping us recover it.

About

Why this work
matters to me.

For most of my life, I believed I could handle anything. And for a long time, I did.

Until life became something I couldn't power through.

I built and sold a multimillion-dollar Amazon brand in under two years, outselling Hallmark and American Greetings in their own category. I grew PureFlix into HubSpot's largest client contact database at 7 million contacts. I reengineered hospital departments at 17. In 2008, while competitors were closing their doors, my agency grew—earning the Make Mine a Million Dollar Business Award through American Express during the financial crisis.

But the real systems challenge came in 2019, when a six-year siege of family crisis began. My father's hospice. My mother's broken leg. A pandemic. My husband's brother diagnosed with terminal cancer. And then, in January 2024, our daughter Eliza was diagnosed with cancer.

I used AI to research treatment protocols, organize the overwhelming logistics of care, and stay present for our son Ollie, who held his sister's hand through all of it. I am not a medical professional. I am a mother who refused to be helpless.

The AI "thinking partners" I built to survive were exactly what every high-performer needs to thrive. I don't just understand systems. I understand the weight of the human carrying them.

Our AI platform is called Oliza, a blend of Ollie and Eliza's names. Eliza rings the bell in May 2026. Every company I've built, every stage I walk onto, every talk I give—it is all for them. That is not a tagline. That is the whole story.
